Act Report

Analysing and visualising data are the final stages to any data analysis process. Analysing the WeRateDogs dataset was a thrilling experience as it allowed us to garner precious insight from the data

The data was gathered from three different sources after which it was cleaned and analysed.

The project was a very fun one as it tested a lot of my skills as a data analyst. Those include:

  • Downloading files programatically
  • Querying an API
  • Data Cleaning/Wrangling
  • Data Analysis
  • Data Visualisation

Insights

Here are some insights I derived from the cleaned master dataset

  • The average rating for a dog is 12/10. It might be shocking that the numerators are bigger than the denominators but WeRateDogs have a peculiar rating system where most ratings are greater than 10.
  • Puppers are the dogs that are rated the most. Although most dogs dont have their stages identified, of those that do, Puppers have the highest count.
    • Puppos have the highest average like per post. I guess people really love their puppos. They show this by liking their posts more than all the others

Some other Insights

I went ahead to plot some information on charts so we can understand the trends so much easier. Come along as I share them with you.

Histogram

This histogram plots the distribution of ratings. We notice that most ratings fall between 1.0 and 1.4

Bar Chart

This bar chart allows us see how many posts each dog type has. We observe visually that Puppers have the highest number of posts.
